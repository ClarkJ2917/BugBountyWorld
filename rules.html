Basic Rules

  1. Try to use real names on slack. People like to communicate with real people.
  2. Do not abuse anyone or offend anyone.😧 If you do so you will be banned from slack. Everyone is here to learn from each other so learn and help learn. 🙂
  3. Use different channels according to their purpose. E.g. Use `#music` channel to talk/share about music ONLY.💃 🕺
  4. Do not pollute the channels with meaningless messages.🤢
  5. Do not bombard anyone’s inbox with messages. They may get annoyed and we don’t want that.😟 Remember, hackers are busy. They have family, work, etc. to do. If you do not get any reply please be patient and continue with your work.
  6. When replying to a specific message or comment in any channel, try to make use of `message threads`.
    Refer: https://get.slack.help/hc/en-us/articles/115000769927-Message-threads
  7. If you are working in a team with other hackers then you can create `private` channel with your buddies and carry on your conversation there.👨‍👧‍👦 This will help lower noise in public channels.
  8. Follow slack basics.

Rules To Follow When Interacting With Platform Employees

  1. When interacting with employees of #BugCrowd, #HackerOne or #Synack, please be polite and respectful.😉 Remember, everyone here is to help each other learn and grow.
  2. Do not abuse anyone.😧
  3. We strive hard to keep #bugbounty community connected and we want you to co-operate and make it easy for researchers to connect with platforms. So, in the end everyone will improve.💃 🕺 💰
